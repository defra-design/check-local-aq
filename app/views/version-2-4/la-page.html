Smoke control areas

Smoke control areas are zones where it’s illegal to emit smoke from a chimney or buy or sell unauthorised fuel for use within them.

Reducing air pollution: local controls

Local councils must have interventions (plans) to reduce air pollution. For example, by:  

  • promoting active travel and public transport 
  • reducing the number of high-polluting vehicles 
  • building a bypass to reduce pollution from through-traffic 
  • introducing one-way systems to keep traffic moving 
  • upgrading buses and taxis to electric or low-emission models 
  • increasing active travel by expanding cycle lanes and improving pedestrian crossings 
  • introducing 'school streets' to restrict traffic during school drop-offs 
  • creating pollution barriers by planting trees and hedges 
  • enforcing smoke control areas to reduce pollution from wood burning stoves 
  • reducing dust and emissions from construction sites
