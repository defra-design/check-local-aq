Long term exposure to air pollution

Long term exposure to air pollution, even if it's low, can increase the risk of getting certain health conditions. The extent of this risk depends on what's in the air and how long you breathe it.

Some of the conditions caused by long term exposure to air pollution are:

  • lung conditions, such as asthma
  • cancer
  • heart disease
  • Alzheimer's disease
  • Dementia
  • Parkinson disease
  • increased respiratory illnesses
