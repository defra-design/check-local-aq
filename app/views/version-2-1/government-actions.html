Local authorities

Local councils and devolved administrations are taking various actions to improve their local air quality.

This includes putting in place:

  • congestion charges, clean air zones and London’s low emission zones (LEZ) and ultra low emission zones (ULEZ) – these all help reduce the amount of traffic and vehicle emissions in busy urban areas
  • lower speed limits, such as 20mph zones
  • smoke control areas, which restrict what you can burn at home
  • school streets, which restrict the use of motor vehicles around schools

For more information about local air quality improvement actions or to report nuisance smoke, waste or smells, contact your local council.

Air Quality Management Areas (AQMAs)

Each local authority in the UK checks air quality in their area against national air quality objectives.

In any area where these objectives are not likely to be met, the local authority must declare an Air Quality Management Area (AQMA). An AQMA could be just a few streets, or it could be a much bigger area.

The local authority will then create a Local Air Quality Action Plan to improve the air quality in the AQMA.

For more information about Local Air Quality Action Plans and any AQMAs in your area, contact your local council.
