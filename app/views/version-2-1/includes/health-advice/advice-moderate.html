For most people, short term exposure to moderate levels of air pollution is not an issue. Continue your usual outdoor activities.

However, some people may experience symptoms of exposure to air pollution. These can start within hours or several days after exposure.

Advice for everyone

You should always try to reduce your exposure to air pollution, but especially if you're experiencing symptoms like:

  • coughing
  • eye, nose, and throat irritation
  • difficulty breathing, chest tightness and pain
  • worse symptoms of existing lung and heart conditions

Find out more about the health effects of air pollution, and what to do if you’re experiencing symptoms.

Advice for people with lung or heart conditions, and older people

You should consider doing lighter exercise and fewer physically demanding activities while air pollution is moderate. This is particularly important for outdoor activities, or if symptoms get worse.

When air pollution is moderate, people with asthma should be prepared to use their reliever inhaler.

Follow your agreed health management plan – for example, an asthma action plan. Ask your doctor or nurse for a plan if you do not have one.

You should also consider other triggers for your symptoms, even when measured air pollution levels are lower. For example, high pollen levels or poor indoor air quality.
