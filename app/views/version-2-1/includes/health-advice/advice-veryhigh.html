Try to reduce your exposure to air pollution, especially if you're experiencing symptoms like:

  • coughing
  • eye, nose, and throat irritation
  • difficulty breathing, chest tightness and pain
  • worse symptoms of existing lung and heart conditions

Consider doing lighter exercise and fewer physically demanding activities, especially outdoors or if your symptoms get worse.

Symptoms can start within hours or several days after exposure.

Find out more about the health effects of air pollution, and what to do if you’re experiencing symptoms.

Advice for people with lung or heart conditions, and older people

You should do less or lighter exercise outdoors while air pollution is very high. Try to do fewer physically demanding activities in general, especially if your symptoms get worse.

When air pollution is very high, people with asthma may need to use their reliever inhaler more often.

Follow your agreed health management plan – for example, an asthma action plan. Ask your doctor or nurse for a plan if you do not have one.

You should also consider other triggers for your symptoms – for example, high pollen levels or poor indoor air quality.
