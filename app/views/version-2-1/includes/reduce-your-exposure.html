Reduce your exposure to air pollution

Long term exposure to air pollution (over years) can lead to serious health conditions and can reduce life expectancy.

You should try to reduce your exposure to air pollution where you can, even when levels are lower. You can:

  • take travel routes with less traffic, especially at busy times of day
  • walk or use public transport where possible
  • compost your garden waste or take it to a refuse centre, instead of burning it
  • improve your indoor air quality by using more efficient appliances and choosing cleaning products marked as “low VOC” (volatile organic compounds)
