Nitrogen dioxide is a brown gas that comes from burning fuels.

When we breathe in a lot of nitrogen dioxide, it irritates the lungs. This can cause inflammation, breathing difficulties and other health effects.

People with existing lung conditions, like asthma, are particularly affected by nitrogen dioxide.

Sources of nitrogen dioxide

Nitrogen dioxide is released when fuels are burned, for example in vehicle engines and industrial processes.
