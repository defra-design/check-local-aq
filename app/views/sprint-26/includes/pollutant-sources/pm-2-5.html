Particulate matter (PM) are small particles of solids or liquids that are in the air. The particles are only 2.5 micrometres in diameter. For context, the width of a human hair is 50 to 70 micrometres. PM2.5 particles may include:

  • dust
  • soot
  • smoke
  • drops of liquid

The main sources of particulate matter are from:

  • burning of fuel by vehicles, industry and domestic properties
  • wear of tyres and brakes
  • wind blown soil and dust
  • sea spray particles
  • burning vegetation
