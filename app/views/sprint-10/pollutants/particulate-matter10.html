Particulate matter (PM) are small particles of solids or liquids that are in the air. The particles are only 10 micrometres in diameter. For context, the width of a human hair is 50 to 70 micrometres.

The main sources of particulate matter are:

  • dust from construction sites
  • dust from landfills
  • dust from agriculture
  • wildfires
  • pollen
  • power plants
  • vehicles

Short term exposure to air pollution can cause:

  • worsening of asthma symptoms
  • worsening of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
  • difficulty breathing
  • coughing
  • eye, nose, and throat irritation
  • chest tightness and pain

Long term health effects of air pollution can include:

  • heart disease
  • diabetes
  • cancer
  • chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
  • Alzheimer's and Parkinson's disease
  • dementia
