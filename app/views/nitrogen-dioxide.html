Sources of nitrogen dioxide

Nitrogen dioxide is a colourless gas. It's mainly produced during:

  • burning of petrol or diesel in a car engine
  • burning natural gas in a central-heating boiler or power station
  • welding
  • commercial manufacturing
  • food manufacturing

Health effects 

  • asthma
  • respiratory infections
  • allergies
  • symptoms of people with lung or heart conditions

Long-term exposure to nitrogen dioxide by children increases their risk of respiratory infection and may lead to poorer lung function in later life.
