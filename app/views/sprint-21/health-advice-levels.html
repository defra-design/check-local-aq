Health advice for people with lung or heart conditions

Adults and children with lung or heart conditions are at greater risk of experiencing symptoms of air pollution (even on days air pollution levels are low).

Follow your doctor's usual advice about physically demanding activities and managing your condition.

Consider other triggers for your symptoms - for example, pollen or indoor air pollution.

Moderate

Continue your usual outdoor activities.

Some people might experience symptoms due to air pollution, even when levels are moderate

Contact your healthcare professional if symptoms continue.

Health advice for people with lung or heart conditions

Adults and children with lung or heart conditions may find their symptoms get worse due to air pollution.

Symptoms could appear within hours or several days later. You should continue to monitor these symptoms for at least a week.

Try to adapt physically demanding activities outdoors if you experience symptoms.

Consider other triggers for your symptoms - for example, pollen or indoor air pollution.

High

Try and reduce your exposure to air pollution, especially if you experience symptoms of air pollution.

Consider other triggers for your symptoms - for example, pollen or indoor air pollution.

Contact your healthcare professional if symptoms continue.

Health advice for people with lung or heart conditions and older people

This advice is for:

  • adults with lung or heart conditions
  • children with lung or heart conditions
  • older people

You should adapt physically demanding activities outdoors, especially if your symptoms get worse.

Symptoms could appear within hours or several days later. You should continue to monitor these symptoms for at least a week.

Very high

Try to adapt physically demanding activities outdoors, especially if you experience symptoms of air pollution.

Contact your healthcare professional if symptoms continue.

Consider other triggers for your symptoms - for example, pollen or indoor air pollution.

Health advice for people with lung or heart conditions and older people

This advice is for:

  • adults with lung or heart conditions
  • children with lung or heart conditions
  • older people

You should adapt physically demanding activities outdoors.

Symptoms could appear within hours or several days later. You should continue to monitor these symptoms for at least a week.
