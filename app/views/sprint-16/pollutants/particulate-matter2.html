Particulate matter (PM) are small particles of solids or liquids that are in the air. The particles are only 2.5 micrometres in diameter. For context, the width of a human hair is 50 to 70 micrometres.

PM2.5 particles may include:

  • dust
  • soot
  • smoke
  • drops of liquid

Due to the small size of these particles, some may enter the bloodstream and travel around the body. For example, entering the heart, brain and other organs.

Sources of PM2.5

Particulate matter can come from nature, human activity, and industrial processes. The main sources of particulate matter are from:

  • burning of fuel by vehicles, industry and domestic properties
  • wear of tyres and brakes
  • wind blown soil and dust
  • sea spray particles
  • burning vegetation

In 2022, 18 per cent of total PM2.5 emissions in the UK were from road transport. Road transport emissions are made up of both exhaust emissions and non-exhaust emissions (brake, tyre and road wear).
