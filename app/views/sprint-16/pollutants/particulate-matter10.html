Particulate matter (PM) are small particles of solids or liquids that are in the air. The particles are only 10 micrometres in diameter. For context, the width of a human hair is 50 to 70 micrometres.

Due to the small size of these particles, some may enter the bloodstream and travel around the body. For example, entering the heart, lungs and other organs.

Sources of PM10

Particulate matter can come from nature, human activity, and industrial processes. The main sources of particulate matter are:

  • dust from construction sites
  • dust from landfills
  • dust from agriculture
  • wildfires
  • pollen
  • power plants
  • vehicles

In 2022, 16 per cent of total PM10 emissions in the UK were from road transport. Road transport emissions are made up of both exhaust emissions and non-exhaust emissions (brake, tyre and road wear).
