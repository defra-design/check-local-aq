Sources of nitrogen dioxide

Nitrogen dioxide is a colourless gas. It's mainly produced during:

  • burning of petrol or diesel in a car engine
  • burning natural gas in a central-heating boiler or power station
  • welding
  • the use of explosives
  • commercial manufacturing
  • food manufacturing

Health effects 

Short term exposure to nitrogen dioxide can cause:

  • asthma attacks
  • respiratory infections
  • symptoms of people with lung or heart conditions could be made worse

Long term exposure to Nitrogen dioxide may lead to:

  • an increased risk of respiratory infection and poorer lung function in children
