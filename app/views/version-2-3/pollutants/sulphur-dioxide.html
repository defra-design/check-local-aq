Sulphur dioxide is colourless gas with an irritating, strong odour. It causes health effects if you come into contact with it, for example:

  • by breathing it in
  • by eating or drinking it
  • through skin contact

Health effects of sulphur dioxide

Breathing in sulphur dioxide can cause irritation of the nose and throat. It is associated with asthma and chronic bronchitis (inflammation of the airways in the lungs).

Skin contact with sulphur dioxide causes stinging pain, blisters and skin redness. Sulphur dioxide can also irritate the eyes and cause eye watering.

Acute exposure to high levels of sulphur dioxide can cause:

  • nausea, stomach pain and vomiting
  • corrosive damage to the airways and lungs
  • eye irritation and blindness

Long term exposure to high levels of sulphur dioxide can lead to:

  • reduced lung function and breathing problems
  • altered sense of smell
  • getting more respiratory infections

Where sulphur dioxide comes from

Sulphur dioxide is released when fuels are burned, both in industry and in households.

How sulphur dioxide is measured

Find out how air pollution is measured and predicted.

Sulphur dioxide pollution is considered:

  • low from 0 to 200 micrograms per cubic metre (µg/m³)
  • moderate from 201 to 400 µg/m³
  • high from 401 to 600 µg/m³
  • very high at 601 µg/m³ or higher
