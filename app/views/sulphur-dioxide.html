Sources of sulphur dioxide

Sulphur dioxide is a colourless gas with a strong odour. It's mainly produced from:

  • burning of petrol or diesel in a car engine
  • gas boilers
  • coal-burning power stations
  • commercial manufacturing
  • food manufacturing

Health effects 

Short-term contact with sulphur dioxide may cause irritation to the eyes, nose, and throat.

With long-term contact at high levels, your lungs may start showing symptoms like coughing or difficulty breathing as it reduces the ability of the lungs to function.
